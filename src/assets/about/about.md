I have always been interested in learning about and interpreting the human condition. As a biomedical researcher, I explored a wide variety of data modalities and methods, ranging from linear regressions for GWAS, clustering and projections for single-cell RNA sequencing, and Bayesian statistics for colocalization. I also practiced many skills in data visualization, user experience, and data infrastructure in order to work with high-dimensional data and communicate complex findings effectively. 

Social media, though not biological, interests me for similar reasons. It impacts us at the same level of importance, driving public opinions and shaping our personal preferences simultaneously as we explore and connect with others digitally. I'm currently getting a taste of the field through my Capstone project, building data scraping pipelines and analysis interfaces to help determine how targeted media campaigns shift public narratives.
